About

Reinforcement learning (RL) is a powerful paradigm for learning from experience and optimizing complex systems. Recent advances in large models have shown remarkable capabilities in understanding and generation on multiple modalities. However, the fact that these models may generate outputs that are inaccurate, inappropriate, or harmful to the human recipients or society remains an issue, and RLHF might play a vital role in improving the performance and alignment of these models by providing them with human feedback that can guide their learning and generation processes. For example, RLHF can help these models to learn from human preferences or evaluations on their outputs, to adapt to human feedback or advice on specific tasks or domains, and to correct their errors or biases based on human corrections or demonstrations. RLHF can also help these models to generate outputs that are more relevant, coherent, diverse, and ethical for the human users and contexts. The goal of this workshop is to bring together researchers and practitioners from different disciplines, such as computer science, psychology, cognitive science, and operations research, to discuss the latest advances and challenges in RLHF. The workshop will cover topics such as:

  • Methods and algorithms for learning from different types of human feedback, such as preferences, demonstrations, corrections, evaluations, and advice
  • Models and metrics for evaluating the quality and reliability of human feedback, and for handling inconsistencies, biases, and noise in human feedback
  • Techniques and tools for eliciting, collecting, and aggregating human feedback from diverse sources, such as crowdsourcing platforms, online platforms, and user studies
  • Ethical and social implications of RLHF, such as ensuring fairness, accountability, transparency, and privacy of the agents and the humans involved
  • Applications and case studies of RLHF in various domains, such as robotics, games, education, health care, and social good
  • Efficiently fine-tune language models with RLHF for new downstream tasks?
We hope to connect researchers from the communities of deep learning, reinforcement learning, language models, and to induce collaborations in this exciting new domain, while providing a platform to discuss recent developments, challenges and tradeoffs.
